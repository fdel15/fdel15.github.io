I am currently working as a Curriculum Engineer at Codecademy. It is a contract to hire position, and the next week and a half will determine if it turns into a full time position for me.

The Ideas

  1. Go through LTC and Ruby tutorials and copy the narratives by hand into a notebook.
    • Will help me become familiar with the Codecademy pattern for writing
  2. Become one with the thesaurus
    • Use a thesaurus adamantly to break down vocabulary words into smaller understandable pieces
  3. Stop being shy
    • Initiate more conversations inside the office
  4. Fake it till you make it
    • Make it a priority to attend all hands on meetings and project demos as if you were already a full time employee.
  5. Listen to feedback
    • Bana and Yael want me to succeed. Pay attention to their feedback and adjust appropriately.
  6. Ask people for their opinions
    • Find people you can trust and ask them if what you wrote is clear. The trick is finding people you can trust to be honest with you.
  7. Finish Bash Tutorial
    • Will help me write tests
  8. Ask for specific feedback
    • Don't assume what your strengths/weaknesses are. Ask what you can do better and make an effort to do better.
