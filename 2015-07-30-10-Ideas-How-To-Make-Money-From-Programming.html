I currently have about $60,000 of debt from student loans. One of the goals when I had when I decided to make the career change to become a programmer was that I would be able to figure out a way to pay off all that debt before I turned 30. It is two months until my 28th birthday so I am running out of time.

The Ideas

  1. Get a Full Time Development job
    • I know this one is obvious but it hasn't happened yet and is still priority number one.
  2. Work on free lancing projects on the side
    • How and where to find free lance clients can be its own ideas list in the future
  3. Win hackathon prizes
    • DevPost (formerly named Challenge Post) always has plenty of online and in person hackathons. Some have really great cash prizes.
    • A great side effect from this strategy is that hackathons are great for networking and building a portfolio
    • Possible future ideas list would be 10 challenge post hackathons that would be fun to enter, 10 possible problems I could solve for the hackathon that I choose to participate in, 10 possible solutions to one of the problems, and 10 ways to recruit a team to help win the hackathon
  4. Tutor students learning to programming
    • This includes doing one or two nights a week at DBC as a coach while working a full time job
  5. Build an online business
    • An online Shopify store with a drop business should be possible.
    • Would be fun to do 10 ideas on products I could drop ship. It would be great to do this during Shopify's next build a business competition.
  6. Dev Bootcamp referral bonuses
    • This isn't official yet, but it looks like they are going to offer alumni some sort of referral bonus for new students.
  7. Build an iPhone app
      8. This post on Medium got me all excited about how you can make a nice profit if you find the right niche and tell a compelling story.
  8. Sell an e-book based on my Bootcamp experience and market it as a helpful guide for future Bootcamp students.
  9. Create a getting started with Arduino guide
    • Would be a great resource for bootcamp graduates trying to learn a new skill beyond web development
    • Would need to learn Arduino first
  10. Build my Equibase database
    • Can use this information to analyze statistics and figure out a way to beat the track
